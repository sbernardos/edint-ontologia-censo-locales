Figure 1 shows the main entities of the ontology and the relationships among them. The ontology represents, among other things, commercial spaces, their aggregations, and their terraces, thereby extending the set of infrastructure elements considered in the EDINT Infrastructure Ontology. Commercial spaces are places prepared for the sale of products, the provision of services, or customer service, such as shops or offices. They may belong to a commercial aggregation, which brings several of them together in a physical location, and have one or more terraces, and they also have an access type. As facilities and, therefore, infrastructure elements, a commercial space, a commercial aggregation, and a terrace have an infrastructure element type and a geolocation, and belong to an administrative division (municipality, district, or neighborhood) (and a zone).

Commercial entities are persons or organizations that carry out or intend to carry out a commercial activity. For this purpose, they may use one or more commercial spaces described above.

Commercial permits have an associated type and an associated status, and are issued to a commercial entity and are applied to a commercial space in order to authorize a specific activity, indicated by a CNAE code and/or an IAE code . Commercial permits may also be applied to terraces in order to authorize a use with a specific surface area, capacity, number of tables, and number of chairs.

NOTE: If any of the terrace data changes, it will be considered a new and different terrace.

This commercial space, named "Cafetería Chamberí", is in active status and belongs to the commercial aggregation commercial aggregation AGR-0001, named "Mercado de Chamberí". Its access type is interior and it has the terrace TER-0001, for which its type, its opening hours, its surface area, its capacity, its number of authorized tables, and its number of authorized chairs are detailed. Data about its location are also included: the administrative division to which it belongs (Trafalgar neighborhood), its postal code (28010), and its geolocation (-3.700200 40.434300) through its geometry.
